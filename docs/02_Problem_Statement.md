# Problem Statement

## Background

Describe the current situation.

Many organizations still manage procurement records through spreadsheets and manual documentation. Many businesses in the Philippines, particularly MSMEs, still rely on manual processes instead of dedicated procurement management systems (Philippine Institute for Development Studies, n.d.; PwC Philippines, 2020).

---

## Existing Problems

### Problem 1

Manual procurement and expense recording increase the risk of discrepancies, errors, and incomplete financial tracking among MSMEs.

Impact:

- Financial records become less accurate.
- Errors are harder to detect and correct.
- Decision-making becomes unreliable.
- Reconciliation takes more time.

### Problem 2

Many MSMEs have limited budgets and few suitable application options, while existing systems are often too complex or feature-heavy for their operational needs.

Impact:

- MSMEs delay or avoid adopting digital tools. 
- Money may be wasted on features they do not need.
- System adoption becomes inefficient.
- Smaller businesses remain stuck with manual processes.

### Problem 3

Many available applications are difficult to learn and use, which increases onboarding time and slows down adoption among MSME users.

Impact:

- Training takes longer.
- Users become frustrated.
- Adoption rates drop.
- Productivity is affected during the adjustment period.

### Problem 4

There is a lack of accessible and affordable applications specifically tailored to the needs of MSMEs.

Impact:

- MSMEs struggle to find a proper fit for their workflow.
- Existing tools feel irrelevant or excessive.
- Operational efficiency remains low
- Businesses may rely on improvised manual methods.

### Problem 5

Many MSMEs struggle to track their expenses effectively, making it difficult to understand where money is being spent and to monitor financial flow.

Impact: 

- Cash flow becomes harder to manage.
- Overspending may go unnoticed.
- Budget planning becomes less effective.
- Profit monitoring becomes inaccurate.

## Proposed Improvement

The proposed system will be a procurement management system made for MSMEs. It will be simpler to use, easier to track expenses with, and more fit for small business workflows.

It will also include onboarding documentation to help new users learn the system faster and reduce confusion during setup.

The system will have two deployment options. One is self-hosted for MSMEs that have someone technical to manage it, and the other is cloud-based for those who want a ready-to-use setup inside the app.

It will also use blockchain-backed verification so every procurement record has an audit trail and cannot be easily altered.
